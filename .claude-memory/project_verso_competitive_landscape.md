**Headline:** FrameReady was discontinued for new feature development in late September 2025. Same parent company owns LifeSaver; users are being migrated. PLAN.md's "FrameReady is the incumbent" framing is stale.

**The 2026 landscape:**
- **LifeSaver** = de facto incumbent. Cloud, opaque subscription (Startup/Core/Plus tiers), strong support, but visualization feature has been broken "all year" per active user complaint. Heavy for a 2-person shop.
- **Virtual Framer** (Lyon, France; $125/mo) = the modern competitor. Native iPad + AI "Design Vault" trained on the framer's style. Positions as a *sales* tool, not a workflow tool. Heavy on visualization + customer-facing proposal UX.
- **FramingPOS** (USA; $30/mo, Square + Google Photos integration) = closest spiritual sibling. Modern, cheap, but POS-framed.
- **SpecialtySoft, FullCalc, ezFramer, GalleryPro, FerenSoft, MarkO, etc.** = mostly Windows desktop legacy, niche or dying.
- **General POS (Square/Lightspeed/Shopify)** = consistently fails for framing because per-job line-item complexity (moulding-by-foot + mat + glass + labor + services) doesn't fit retail POS line-item models. This is the same objection Phil voiced about his prior failed POS.

**Verso's positioning thesis (v1, pressure-test before Phil visit):**
*"The first framing tool built for the back-of-the-shop workflow, not the front counter. Your paper tickets scan in by photo, your framer sees the same spec the customer signed, and you don't pay for a POS you didn't ask for."*

Three pillars:
1. **Ticket-first, not POS-first.** Every competitor is a POS that grew framing features. Verso is a digital paper ticket that hands off to the framer.
2. **iPad-native, no install.** PWA → Add to Home Screen. LifeSaver is cloud but not iPad-native; Virtual Framer is iPad-native but $125/mo and sales-focused.
3. **Migration in minutes.** Claude-vision OCR scan of paper tickets. No competitor has this; it's the switching-cost-killer feature.

**Concede honestly (in pitch, not as gaps to fill):** no visualization, no vendor catalog auto-update, no QuickBooks integration, no multi-shop, no retail inventory, no payment processing.

**Pricing anchor:** target $39–$59/mo for the one-shop tier — undercuts Virtual Framer 3x, 30% above FramingPOS to signal a step up in focus. Free pilot for Phil; full pricing analysis is Workstream D.

**Why this is in memory:** the discontinuation news + positioning thesis are the kind of facts that should NOT have to be re-derived every session. They're durable for at least the next 6 months.
